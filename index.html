For Brendan · built from our full thread (27,500+ messages, Feb 2023→now) and how Amedeo actually answers · ordered by what you ask most
THE GOLDEN RULE — Try to solve it every way you know first. Only text Amedeo when the answer is genuinely his call (the amber list below). When you do: answer-first, one line.
⚠️ THE #1 THING — kill the "is that fine?" reflex. Looking back at everything, 6 of every 10 questions you ask are confirmation — "is that fine? · right? · or no? · what should we do here? · should I tell him?" Almost every time, the answer is yes, do it. New rule: if you're about to ask "is that fine?" — it's fine. Do it, then tell me what you did in one line. Send me a decision, not a question: "Edwin's covering the Calverton, posted the Westbury at $500" beats "should I have Edwin do Calverton and post Westbury?"

The Boundary — handle it yourself vs text Amedeo

✅ YOU decide (don't text me)

  • Telling a carrier the rate — it's in the table, just read it to them
  • Driver ETAs / tracking chase — call the driver
  • Relaying a routine ETA to a flexible customer (Newburgh, etc.)
  • Assigning a known driver to a standard lane at the standard rate
  • Posting an open load on DAT (price high)
  • Routing a contact — email vs text vs group chat (see matrix)
  • Tire repairs (route by equipment owner)
  • Temp checks/adjustments vs the BOL setpoint (incl. 1° mismatches)
  • Trailer swaps; sending the Excel BOL (never the delivery order)
  • Finding a "lost" trailer (look up the container #)
  • Yard parking / drops / fuel-list prep · Masport + Rose Rocket updates
  • Pickup # not working → run the tree before me

🟡 ASK Amedeo (these are mine)

  • Paying above the recovery max, or booking at a loss
  • Booking a brand-new / unknown carrier
  • Any TONU — never offered without my OK
  • Any fuel advance (those go through Bailey)
  • Whether to deliver a non-running trailer
  • Customer-facing messaging about a delay/problem (esp. John @ Glow) — what we tell a customer is my call
  • Fines & payment disputes (incl. holding a driver's pay)
  • Which loads to prioritize when we can't cover them all

⚡ Fast Answers — ordered by how often you ask

Tap a question. The number is roughly how many times it's come up.

Should I email them, text them, or hit the group chat?~295×
See the Who-to-Contact matrix below. Defaults: delays/problems → EMAIL the customer (Glow, Dole, Chiquita, Top Banana) so there's a record they can't deny. Routine driver coordination → text/group chat is fine. Don't call John @ Glow unprepared — loop me first. You almost never need to ask me which channel — pick email if it's a problem, text if it's routine.
Who's covering [lane]? / Should I post it? / Anyone near there?~267×
If it's unassigned + standard → assign a known driver yourself (see Carrier Bench). Should I post it? → YES, post it high, don't ask. Nobody confirmed by 8pm → run the Coverage tree (blast everyone, DAT, double-book). Only text me if it hits the amber list (loss / above max / unknown carrier / priority call).
A driver / trailer is "missing" at the yard — what do I do?~196×
YOU HANDLE Don't ask me — find it: 1) Look up the container # in Masport to see its real status/location. 2) Check the other spots — OEX, Leonard's, Garasches, the back area of the yard. 3) A port puller may have dropped it somewhere else — check who pulled it. 4) Have the driver photograph what's actually there and read you numbers. Tell drivers which yard has space for drops. Active yards: 56/76 J Caldwell (main), Garasches (overflow/Everett), OEX/Leonard's (port).
What's the rate? — a carrier's asking me what we pay.~111×
YOU HANDLE It's in the rate table — just tell them. You don't need me to relay a standard rate. Everett ~$1,634 round trip (~$1,500 delivery leg). Calverton/Westbury ~$1,270. Newburgh ~$956. New Haven ~$1,200. Posting a spot? Price HIGH to guarantee coverage. Only ask me if they want more than a few hundred over these, or you'd be booking at a loss.
Is this load coming out of the port — today or tomorrow?port
Check the pickup date on the port list / Masport — don't send a driver before the pickup date. If it says picked up tomorrow, it goes tomorrow (unless I've said pull early). Storm/port-delay days: not everything comes out, and the customers know it. Pickup # not working → run the tree.
Driver gave me an ETA — do I pass it to the customer?~75×
Routine ETA to a flexible customer (Newburgh, etc.) → yes, relay it, you don't need me. But if the ETA means a delay or a missed window (esp. Glow/Everett, Top Banana) → that becomes a customer-delay message = mine, and it goes in email. Driver stopped on tracking → call him now, don't wait.
Is the Excel sheet the BOL? Do I send the delivery order too?~51×
YOU HANDLE The Excel sheet IS the BOL. Drivers get only that. Never send the delivery order — "it's worthless at delivery." IFFC/Everett drivers must have the Excel BOL in hand. Need a customer PO that's not on it → it's usually on the Excel sheet (Glow PO starts 818-ish); if missing, ask the customer.
A trailer won't start / genset / battery / keeps shutting off.~91×
MOSTLY YOU Run the Mechanical tree. Whose equipment? Chiquita → TTS/Goodyear (they pay direct). Dole → email for a work-order # FIRST. Leased → JD/Yeisi. Glad hands at Everett → ask John @ Glow. Port delivered it damaged → document + bill back to the port carrier. Only the non-running delivery decision is mine.
An empty / wrong trailer came back — is it ours? Do we pay him?~26×
Never bring back an empty that isn't ours (no exceptions). Cross-check the # vs the Masport empty list + the customer's empty-list email. Dole↔Chiquita can't interchange. Wrong trailer brought back → payment is held / fine applies — but holding a driver's pay is my call, so flag that one. "Can the return trailer be a load-out?" — only if it's the right type & ours.
Temp on the BOL doesn't match the trailer — fine?~18×
YOU HANDLE Match the BOL setpoint. A 1° gap (57 vs 58) is fine. Bananas 57–58°F, pineapple/lime 45°F (7.2°C), mixed-with-banana usually banana temp (Newburgh = 52°F overnight). Glow/Everett: don't preset — match the Gate Pass, flag a mismatch. Keep a non-running trailer at temp by plugging into an adjacent genset. Deliver-not-running = my call.
Which trailers do I fuel / mark "do not fill"?~49×
YOU HANDLE Build the morning list for Gabe/Antoine, every trailer accessible. Ayco → top them all off. Hatfield → +$50 top-off. Going back to the carrier/TMX → mark "do not fill." Omari fuel receipts/advances → through Bailey, not you.
What's my schedule — am I in Sunday / the holiday?~37×
📌 Amedeo — set the standing schedule here so this stops being asked: "Brendan works ____ nights/week, ____–____. Holidays: work unless told otherwise. Sundays: ____."
Default until set: if there's a real route list, work it — holidays included. Only ping me if the list looks empty.

A driver fell off / a load has no coverage

⏰ Past 8pm with no confirmed driver? → Sound the alarm.
1️⃣ Blast your active bench at once (see Carrier Bench): Jes-Haulers, Chapp, TMX3, Concord, Jxf, Prime Warrior (Lewin), 56 Nights, Nieves, Isdani, Warhorse. Match the lane to who runs it.

3️⃣ Call drivers already on routes — anyone nearby who can grab an extra?
4️⃣ Post on DAT — price HIGH (~$1,600+ Everett now). New carrier → MC# + RMIS packet.
5️⃣ Backup found but original might still show? → Double-book (standard hedge). First to the yard gets it; give the other a different load. Don't tell either they're backup.
🟡 Only cover is above max / at a loss, or needs a new carrier? → text me one line. Critical + can't reach me → lock someone in anyway, fix the number after.
